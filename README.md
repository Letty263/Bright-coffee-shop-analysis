Bright Coffee Shop is a growing café chain that has embarked on a journey to better understand its sales performance and customer behavior. With a new CEO at the helm, the company is focused on leveraging data to drive revenue growth, improve operational efficiency and enhance the overall customer experience. This project was designed to analyze historical sales data and provide actionable insights that can guide strategic decision‑making.

The primary objective of the project is to uncover trends and patterns in sales transactions. By examining product performance, customer preferences and time‑based variations, the analysis aims to identify which items are most popular, which times of day or days of the week generate the highest sales and which categories may be underperforming. These insights are critical for optimizing inventory, staffing and promotional strategies.

The dataset used in this project contains detailed information about sales transactions, including product names, quantities sold, prices and timestamps. It also includes store‑level details such as branch locations and staff identifiers and in some cases, customer information such as loyalty status or payment method. Time variables like day of the week, hour of sale, and month are particularly important for identifying seasonal trends and peak demand periods.

To conduct the analysis, a combination of tools and technologies were employed. SQL was used to extract, clean, and aggregate the raw data, ensuring accuracy and consistency. Excel was leveraged to create interactive dashboards that present the findings in a clear and accessible format. Planning and workflow mapping were supported by visual tools such as Miro.

The workflow followed a structured process. It began with data inspection, where missing values, duplicates, and inconsistencies were identified. Data cleaning followed, ensuring that the dataset was reliable and ready for analysis. Exploratory analysis was then conducted to reveal sales by product, time and location, as well as customer behavior patterns. From these insights, performance evaluations were made to highlight best‑selling items, underperforming categories, and branch comparisons. Finally, recommendations were developed to guide the CEO in making informed decisions, such as introducing product bundles, adjusting staffing schedules, enhancing loyalty programs, and implementing targeted promotions.

The key deliverables of the project include a cleaned and structured dataset, SQL scripts for core queries, visual dashboards, and an executive summary report. Together, these outputs provide a comprehensive view of Bright Coffee Shop’s sales performance and offer practical strategies for growth. Looking ahead, future enhancements could include integrating customer feedback, developing predictive models for demand forecasting, and building real‑time analytics dashboards for store managers.

In conclusion, the Bright Coffee Shop Sales Analytics Project demonstrates the power of data in shaping business strategy. By transforming raw sales records into meaningful insights, the project equips leadership with the knowledge needed to make confident, data‑driven decisions. This initiative will not only supports immediate revenue growth but also lays the foundation for sustainable success in a competitive market.
